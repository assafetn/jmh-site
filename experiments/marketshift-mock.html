The lump-sum era.

A quiet industry change in how companies fund relocation created a gap no one planned for.

For decades, companies moving employees abroad handled everything — housing search, visa filings, tax support, vendor relationships. Then, under cost pressure, the industry shifted. Today, roughly 40% of companies simply hand employees a lump sum and step back.

The employee now has a budget. What they don't have is the infrastructure, the expertise, or the vendor relationships the relocation company used to provide. That gap is new. It has no name in most HR systems.

The gap this created

Prosumer Mobility Gap

The space between having a budget and having a system. The employee has purchasing power but none of the infrastructure they've just been handed responsibility for.

† Worldwide ERC / Brookfield Global Relocation Trends, 2024. $22B global relocation market.
